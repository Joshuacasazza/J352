This may be the most important offseason in the Jets history. The team has a lot of money to spend and a lot of wholes to fill.

The Jets have made a splash to start free agency. The Jets started by bringing in OG Laken Tomlinson. Tomlinson is 30 years old and earned his first Pro-Bowl selection last year.

TE C.J. Uzomah was the next signing for the Jets. The team signed him to a 3 year, 24 million dollar deal.

"Don't underestimate C.J. Uzomah's upbeat leadership on a very young team. Steal for the @nyjets. Big plays coming to NYJ," said former NFL WR Cris Collinsworth on Twitter.

The Jets then turned to the defensive side of the football. They signed D.J. Reed from the Seahawks. Reed, a 25 year old CB, had his best season of his career with Seattle in 2021.

Continuing to add to their secondary, the Jets signed former Bucs SS Jordan Whitehead. The 24 year old instantly becomes the best safety on the roster.
